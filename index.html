About Me

Welcome! I'm Hasan C. Öztürk, a game developer and narrative designer based in Germany. My passion lies at the intersection of storytelling and interactive systems, where I explore how player agency shapes experiences.

After earning my BA in Interdisciplinary American Studies (Tübingen) and my MA in Digital Humanities (Stuttgart), both with high grades (1.9 and 1.8 respectively), I've focused on multivariate narration in games. My academic work examined environmental storytelling and multivariate narration in new media (ergodic literature and meaningful choice). While studying, I also completed a Unity VR course exploring spatial narrative techniques, created smaller games in Lua, and learned Python and web development (HTML/CSS/JS/Flask). I earned certificates in CS50x: Introduction to Computer Science and GAD110.3x: Introduction to Game Design – Game Systems and Balance (links below).

Beyond academia, I actively create games and stories: developing The Catacombs of Cathruban (a Twine text adventure), leading tabletop RPG sessions for Tübingen clubs (DAI and Troll Tübingen), and crafting original quests, items, and monsters with pen and paper as well as computer graphics software. My technical projects include Ghost Hunter (CS50 final project – try it below) and 3D creature modeling in Blender, building on my lifelong drawing practice.

Currently, I'm advancing my Unity and Lua skills while also pursuing SCRUM Master certification. I'm particularly interested in narrative systems that adapt to player choices while maintaining compelling dramatic arcs.

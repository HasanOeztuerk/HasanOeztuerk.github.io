About Me

Welcome! I'm Hasan C. Öztürk, a game developer and narrative designer based in Germany. My passion lies at the intersection of storytelling and interactive systems, where I explore how player agency shapes experiences.

After earning my BA in Interdisciplinary American Studies (Tübingen) and my MA in Digital Humanities (Stuttgart), I've focused on multivariate narration in games. My academic work examined environmental storytelling and multivariate narration in new media (ergodic literature and meaningful choice). While studying, I also completed a Unity VR course exploring interactivity in VR space, created smaller games in Lua, and learned Python and web development (HTML/CSS/JS/Flask). I earned certificates in CS50x: Introduction to Computer Science and GAD110.3x: Introduction to Game Design – Game Systems and Balance.

Beyond academia, I actively create games and stories: developing The Catacombs of Cathruban (a Twine text adventure), leading tabletop RPG sessions for roleplaying groups and clubs, and crafting original quests, items, and monsters with pen and paper as well as computer graphics software. My technical projects include Ghost Hunter (CS50 final project – try it below) and 3D creature modeling in Blender, building on my lifelong drawing practice.

Currently, I'm advancing my Unity and coding skills while also pursuing a SCRUM Master certification. I'm particularly interested in narrative systems that adapt to player choices while maintaining compelling dramatic arcs.

I’m deeply fascinated by nature, history, and diverse global cultures—a perspective rooted in my own multicultural background. To me, the world is a tapestry of interconnected experiences, and I strive to weave this richness into my design work by embracing varied lenses and perspectives. Understanding motivations, cultures, and languages is central to my creative philosophy, and merging these insights with my lifelong love for video games is what drives me.

About Me

Welcome! I'm Hasan C. Öztürk, a game developer and narrative designer based in Germany. My passion lies at the intersection of storytelling and interactive systems, where I explore how player agency shapes experiences.

After earning my BA in Interdisciplinary American Studies (Tübingen) and MA in Digital Humanities (Stuttgart) both with honors, I've focused on multivariate narration in games. My academic work examined environmental storytelling and flow states, culminating in a Unity VR demo exploring spatial narrative techniques.

Beyond academia, I actively create games and stories: developing The Catacombs of Cathruban (a Twine text adventure), leading tabletop RPG sessions for Tübingen clubs (DAI and Troll Tübingen), and crafting original quests, items, and monsters. My technical projects include Ghost Hunter (CS50 final project try it down below) and 3D creature modeling in Blender, building on my lifelong drawing practice.

Currently advancing my Unity and Lua skills while pursuing SCRUM Master certification, I'm particularly interested in narrative systems that adapt to player choices while maintaining compelling dramatic arcs.
